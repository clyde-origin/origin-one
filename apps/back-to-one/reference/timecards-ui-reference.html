Notes on what's shown. Frame A uses a per-department grouped grid — matches the existing Crew roster structure (same department groupings, same order). Hours appear in the day cell; a colored dot below indicates status (teal=approved, amber=reopened, indigo=submitted, gray=draft). Reopened cells are backlit amber for at-a-glance queue spotting. Frame B is what a producer sees when they tap Ryan's row — the identity block at top, week selector, then seven day-rows below. Empty days are low-contrast; days with entries get a card. The reopened entry carries the full context: hours, status, description, the reopen reason (with attribution), and crew would see an "Edit entry" CTA here (not shown, producer view).

What a crew member sees on Frame B is structurally identical but with an Add hours affordance on empty days and Edit entry on their own reopened/draft entries (not on approved ones — those are locked).
